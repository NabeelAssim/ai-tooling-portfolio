## Post 1 - [Posted on 30th June, 2026]
If I had to sign a new client ASAP, I would:

(incoming list of my 5 proven tactics)

1. Revive old conversations in your LinkedIn DMs - you'd be surprised how many leads are just "sitting" without proper motivation to message you first.

2. Write posts which include client quotes as a form of social proof, or results, whenever possible. I wouldn't call these "sales" posts. These could be any type of post with a problem-solving angle. (like today's post)

3. Get on a LinkedIn Live stream - demonstrate your expertise in real time, and then sell ON the call.

4. Message your profile viewers - when someone views your profile, that is already a "warm" signal. So start those conversations casually. Just don't pitchslap.

5. Write the best comments ever - on others' posts. Contribute. Add! And yes, it's true that a single "good" comment can bring you more profile views than any of the above combined. Comments are content too!

## Post 2 - [Posted on 29th June, 2026]
I'm often asked about my LinkedIn "early days".

But I rarely get asked this one question:

"What would you NOT change in your LinkedIn journey, if you had it to do all over again?"

(Finally, thank you for asking, you 😊)

The one thing I'd never change is my rule and opinion on "daily posting". Daily is simply not necessary.

I'm not saying you shouldn't.
I'm saying I wouldn't. Ever.

Here's my LinkedIn post schedule over the years:

1. In my early days, I did 4-5 posts per week.
2. Later, I've gone to 3-4 posts per week.
3. These days, I do 2-3 posts per week.

Fun fact: I've only ever tried 6 posts per week for two weeks. That's how long I managed to do it. Simply not sustainable. (if quality is your thing)

## Post 3 - [Posted a week ago]
Every client I coach gets 3 "musts" from me.

To share, showcase, and sell. (hear me out)

1. I don't care whether you share 100% of what you know. Or 50% of it, and then let people pay for it. Fine, your choice. But you MUST share your hard-earned knowledge with others. 1 post = 1 topic = 1 takeaway.

(Personally, I always say "share it all")

2. Showcase your work. A lot of beginners on LinkedIn would swap their career with yours in a heartbeat. So why aren't you talking about your results? You MUST.

(My entire profile is riddled with testimonials)

3. Sell your product. You MUST. Nobody else will do it for you. I coach. I speak. I have a community. So I talk about it a lot. But the real skill in "selling" is how elegantly you can do it, without it feeling like a pitch.

(I always "sell without selling", as I did in this post)

Remember my 3 musts: Share. Showcase. Sell.
