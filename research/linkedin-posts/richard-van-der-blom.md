# Richard van der Blom - LinkedIn Posts

## Post 1 - [Posted on 3rd July, 2026]
The algorithm is changing fast, so we stepped up the pace of our research as well. 5 Trends worth knowing, based on +12,000 creators since March.

The first one I noticed myself, down -22% for my own content.

1. Carousels are tanking.

Our analysis of 40,000+ carousel posts shows average reach is down 40%, and engagement has dropped by 30%. LinkedIn is making room for video, and your audience is done swiping through low-value slides.

2. Deleting posts hurts more than it helps.

Got a typo, wrong visual, or just underperforming content? Unless it's truly off-brand, let it live. Try to resuscitate it. Deleting doesn't erase it from your history; it signals instability to the algorithm.

3. Your connections are dragging down your reach.

Want to grow fast? Build followers. But be ruthless about who you connect with. If they don't post, don't engage, and sit outside your topic fingerprint, they'll quietly tank your distribution.

4. Spamming DMs is killing your visibility.

If people keep ignoring your messages, LinkedIn notices. Low response rates on your outbound DMs signal spam behavior, and the platform will suppress your profile and content. Honestly, I'm cheering for this one.

5. Comment-and-run is getting penalized.

Leave 30 comments and disappear? LinkedIn now tracks whether you follow up. If someone replies to your comment and you ghost them, our data shows your comment reach can drop by 35%. Your relevance ranking tanks.

The algorithm rewards behavior that looks like genuine participation.
Performative tactics are quietly dying, and honestly, that's a good thing!

## Post 2 - [Posted on 1st July, 2026]
Finally LinkedIn in 2026 is punishing lazy virality. I remember a quickly shot video in my feed with close to no value reaching half a million views. Bye bye.

Your follower count is becoming irrelevant.
Your engagement is quietly collapsing.
And your workload keeps growing.

You know me, I've been tracking this shift:

Most entrepreneurs on LinkedIn are burning out because they're still optimizing for visibility, not results.

You never have an algorithm problem, it's always about intention.

LinkedIn has shifted from professional social network to intent graph. The platform now rewards invisible interactions (like clicks, saves, profile visits, DMs) far more than performative engagement.

The more you chase likes, the more you train the algorithm (and your audience) to see you as free entertainment instead of a decision partner.

If you're building a business here, that's lethal.

3 moves to stop burning out while LinkedIn gets stricter:

→ 1. Stop feeding the feed. Build assets instead.
Treat each post as a reusable knowledge object, not a one-off opinion. Carousels, frameworks and checklists that people can save and apply will outperform hot takes over time. If a post wouldn't belong in your playbook, don't publish it.

→ 2. Measure silent intent as your primary KPI.
Start tracking what actually moves the business: clicks to your site, DM replies, call bookings, profile visits from your ICP. If a post explodes in likes but generates zero intent, declare it a failure. The algorithm already does.

→ 3. Design a sustainable rhythm, not a heroic sprint.
Pick a narrow promise for your audience (aka one problem, one transformation) and show up consistently in that lane. Build recurring formats you can execute on autopilot instead of reinventing yourself every day for the feed.

LinkedIn 2027 will be brutal on people who still act like creators instead of operators.

The platform is evolving into a work operating system, not a vanity arena.

Your job is no longer to win the feed.
Your job is to turn attention into outcomes, without living on the platform or copying the wrong strategies.

## Post 4 [Posted a week go]
LinkedIn just made a small but smart change. In your feed, engagement numbers now show as symbols instead of text.
 
👍 180 💬 144 🔁 2
 
Clean. Fast. Less cluttered.
So? Good move.
 
But since we're already tweaking the engagement experience... 
LinkedIn, I have a wishlist.
 
You have the data. You just need the UI.
 
Here are 5 features that would actually change how creators use this platform:
 
1. Show me who saved my post 
↳ Saves are your strongest reach signal. A post with 10+ saves reaches a median of 2,085% more than one with zero. Yet you hide who saved it completely. Give me names. Let me start a real conversation.
 
2. Show me engagement streaks 
↳ Who has liked, commented or shared 3, 5, 10 of my posts in a row? That person is not a passive follower. That's a warm lead. Possibly a buyer. Flag them. Make them visible.
 
3. Show me multiple-action engagers 
↳ Who liked AND commented AND shared the same post? That triple signal is the highest intent a follower can show without sending a DM. I want to know who they are.
 
4. Suggest connections based on the above 
↳ You already recommend people I might know. Start recommending people who have been quietly telling me they're interested. Turn the algorithm into a sales assistant.
 
5. Show me which companies are engaging repeatedly 
↳ Not just individuals. Which organisations are sending multiple people to my content? That's account-level intent data sitting unused. B2B creators would pay for that insight alone.
 
Bonus 6. Let me see engagement trends over time per follower 
↳ Did someone go from liking one post a month to three a week? Warming up. Show me the trajectory, not just the snapshot.
 
LinkedIn already has this data.
 
The gap between what they track and what they share with creators is still huge.
